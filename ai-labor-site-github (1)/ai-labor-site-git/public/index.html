A toy model, not a forecast. For mind and heart jobs, AI capability follows a Gompertz curve fixed by two points: the share of tasks automatable today and your guess for five years out. For hand jobs, capability follows a fixed embodied-AI curve gated by when dexterous robots arrive (the slider you control). Heart-job cores are never automatable — only their support work is, and the human core grows with the market. Employment is shown indexed to 100 = today's workforce. As capability rises, AI substitutes for human labor while a falling price expands the market. Layoffs are fast (≈0.75 yr); hiring is slower. The dashed gold line is the wage index per remaining worker: it rises when survivors become scarcer and more productive (AI leverage), and falls once AI can do the core task cheaply, competing pay down toward the cost of the AI itself.
